About Malappuram

Malappuram, gracefully spread between the majestic Western Ghats and the serene Arabian Sea, is a district that breathes history, culture, and unmatched spirit. It’s a land where the pulse of tradition echoes in the sounds of Mappila songs, the grandeur of historic mosques and temples, and the energy of football grounds filled with roaring fans. Often called the ‘Football Capital of Kerala’, Malappuram’s love for the game isn’t just a pastime — it’s an emotion stitched into everyday life. From the Nercha festivals to boat races at Biyyam Kayal, every event here carries the essence of its diverse communities. Explore its scenic hills, sacred sites, bustling towns, and the warmth of people rooted in art, poetry, and resilience. Whether you're a history buff, nature lover, or someone chasing the thrill of a local football match, Malappuram will welcome you with open arms and leave a lasting impression.

Top Destinations in Malappuram

Heritage & Religious Sites

Pazhayangadi Mosque

A centuries-old mosque with rich architecture and spiritual relevance.

Known for its blend of Kerala and Persian styles, this mosque is a hub of learning and tradition in Malappuram.

Nature & Scenic Spots

Kottakkunnu

A historic hill garden offering panoramic views and cultural events.

This scenic spot offers breathtaking views, an open-air theater, and food stalls, making it a favorite family destination.

View on Google Maps

Museums & Educational Attractions

Kottakkal Arya Vaidya Sala Museum

Preserving the legacy of Ayurveda and Kerala’s healing traditions.

This museum showcases ancient ayurvedic tools, manuscripts, and the rich history of Kottakkal’s contribution to medicine.

Events & Festivals

Biyyam Boat Race August

Held at Biyyam Kayal in Ponnani, this vibrant boat race is a visual spectacle celebrating Malappuram’s connection to water and tradition.

Mamankam Festival Historic

Though no longer celebrated, Mamankam on the banks of Bharathapuzha remains a legendary part of Malappuram’s past — a grand festival of warriors and legacy.

Malappuram Nercha March–April

A unique Islamic festival involving processions, offerings, and traditional music, Nercha is an emotional, spiritual gathering tied to Malabar’s soul.

Mappila Paattu Evenings All Year

Cultural nights featuring traditional Mappila songs, often during religious or community gatherings, showcasing lyrical heritage and history.

Local Cuisine

Pathiri & Chicken Curry

Available at: Tharavad, Mappila Bhavan

Soft rice flatbreads paired with rich, spicy chicken curry cooked Mappila-style.

Unnakaya

Available at: Local bakeries and homes

Fried banana stuffed with coconut, jaggery, and cardamom — a sweet tradition.

Football Culture in Malappuram

Football isn’t just a sport here — it’s a pulse, a lifestyle, and an unspoken language of unity and fire.

"In Malappuram, kids don't learn to walk — they learn to dribble." – Local Coach, Kottakkal

Sevens Football

Locally organized 7-a-side matches fill the nights with cheering crowds and raw talent. It's the soul of grassroots football in Malabar.

Malappuram District League

A breeding ground for Kerala’s best — players rise from dusty fields to I-League and ISL trials.

Community Tournaments

Every town has its tournament. Whether it’s Kottappadi or Manjeri, football festivals unite everyone from school kids to grandmothers.
